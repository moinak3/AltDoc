Problem

Great ideas get stuck between speaking and writing.

The most original ideas often show up before they are organized enough to write. Voice makes them easy to capture, but hard to structure, evaluate, and turn into a finished document.

Notes live in silos

Voice memos, transcripts, reading reactions, and meeting thoughts pile up without a shared project structure.

It's hard to see what matters

Strong ideas are mixed with repetition, uncertainty, loose references, and unsupported jumps.

Drafting starts too early

Generic writing tools can make incomplete thinking sound finished before the argument is actually ready.
